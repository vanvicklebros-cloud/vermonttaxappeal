## Why Commercial Properties Are Overvalued in Orange County

### Mixed-Use Property Income Capitalization Errors

Properties combining residential, office, and retail uses are systematically overvalued using combined-use income assumptions:

**The error:**
1. **Simultaneous occupancy assumption** – Listers assume residential, office, and retail uses operate at 100% occupancy simultaneously
2. **Blended cap rate misapplication** – Using single-use cap rates (5–6%) for mixed-use properties instead of appropriate blended rates (7–9%)
3. **Inflated combined revenue** – Assuming all uses generate stabilized income when actual operations show sequential use or partial occupancy
4. **Ignored management complexity** – Overvaluing properties requiring complex multi-tenant management

**Real-world example:** A 3-unit building (residential upstairs, retail downstairs, office):
- **Lister calculation:** ($12K residential + $8K retail + $5K office) = $25K annual income ÷ 5.5% cap = $454K
- **Market reality:** Property values at $280K based on recent sales in similar Orange County towns
- **Overvaluation:** $174K (62% above market)

### Manufacturing & Processing Facility Overvaluation

Orange County's small-scale manufacturers and processing facilities are overvalued using inflated income and production assumptions:

- **Commodity price assumptions** – Using historical high prices instead of current market rates
- **Production capacity misvaluation** – Listers assess properties "as if" fully utilized; actual utilization is 60–70%
- **Comparable sales ignored** – Lack of recent sales of similar facilities forces Listers to use national income matrices
- **Regional benchmark misapplication** – Using larger-facility benchmarks for small, specialized operations

### Historic Property Premium Inflation

Historic buildings and properties in designated historic districts receive excessive valuation premiums:

- Historic designation doesn't inherently increase income or value unless property is actively managed for preservation
- Listers apply 10–15% "historic premium" with no corresponding income support
- Preservation restrictions actually reduce flexibility and increase costs, but Listers ignore this

## The Vermont Grievance & Appeal Process: Your Pathway to a Reduction

Vermont law (32 V.S.A. § 4111) guarantees all property owners the right to challenge Grand List assessments. Here's the exact timeline for Orange County:

### Step 1: The Grand List Filing (April 1)

Your town Listers file the completed Grand List by April 1. You're entitled to inspect it at your town office.

### Step 2: Grievance Filing (30 days from Grand List)

Within 30 days of Grand List filing, file a **written grievance** with your town Listers. Orange town deadlines vary:
- **Randolph:** May 8 (2026)
- **Chelsea:** May 7 (2026)
- **Williamstown:** May 6 (2026)
- **Bradford:** May 9 (2026)

**Your grievance must include:**
- Property identification (Grand List number, street address)
- Statement of alleged valuation or classification error
- Supporting evidence (comps, condition reports, income documentation)
- Requested assessed value

**We prepare and file your grievance.** You provide property details and supporting evidence.

### Step 3: Listers' Hearing (60–90 days after grievance)

Town Listers schedule a formal hearing. Your opportunity to present evidence; Listers must provide written findings.

### Step 4: Board of Civil Authority (BCA) Appeal (14 days after denial)

You have **14 days** to appeal to your town's **Board of Civil Authority** (select board).

**BCA standard:** You must prove by clear and convincing evidence that Listers' valuation is wrong.

### Step 5: Director of Property Valuation and Review (State Appeal)

If BCA denies your appeal, appeal to Vermont's **Director** (state level).

**State appeal standard:** De novo review; Director weighs evidence independently.

## Winning Grievance Evidence: What Persuades Listers & the BCA

### Comparable Sales Analysis (Most Powerful)

**Residential properties:** Find 3–5 recent arm's-length sales of similar properties in the same neighborhood, sold within 12 months. Adjust for square footage, age, condition, lot size, views, renovation recency.

**Orange opportunity:** Rural Orange County properties sell slowly with significant discounts from asking. Document listing-to-sale ratios; a property listed at $310K but sold at $280K directly contradicts a $305K assessment.

**Commercial properties:** Recent sales of similar mixed-use, manufacturing, or hospitality properties are decisive. Limited comps data in Orange makes any recent sale powerful evidence.

### Income and Expense Documentation

**For mixed-use properties:**
- Separate lease agreements for each use (residential, retail, office)
- Actual rental income from each use (not assumed)
- Operating expenses specific to each use
- Proof of actual occupancy rates (occupancy logs, payment records)

**For manufacturing/processing facilities:**
- 2–3 years of actual tax returns (Schedule C, Form 1065)
- Production data showing actual output, not nameplate capacity
- Commodity prices documenting price declines vs. Lister assumptions
- Actual utilization rates and downtime documentation

**For historic properties:**
- Cost documentation for preservation compliance and maintenance
- Market analysis showing historic designation premium is zero or negative

### Professional Appraisals

A certified MAI appraisal (cost: $1,500–$3,500) carries tremendous weight. Ensure the appraiser has experience with rural Vermont mixed-use and manufacturing properties.

### Condition and Physical Deficiency Reports

Document deferred maintenance:
- Roof age and replacement cost
- HVAC, plumbing, electrical issues
- Foundation or structural damage
- Contractor quotes for necessary repairs

### Lister Methodology Errors

**For mixed-use (income capitalization):**
- Prove that Listers assumed simultaneous occupancy of all uses (unrealistic)
- Show actual occupancy rates are lower than assumed
- Demonstrate cap rate used is inappropriate for mixed-use properties
- Show comparable properties sell at higher cap rates
