## The Vermont Grievance & Appeal Process: Your Pathway to a Reduction

Vermont law (32 V.S.A. § 4111) guarantees all property owners the right to challenge Grand List assessments. Here's the exact timeline for Orleans County:

### Step 1: The Grand List Filing (April 1)

Your town Listers file the completed Grand List by April 1. You're entitled to inspect it at your town office.

### Step 2: Grievance Filing (30 days from Grand List)

Within 30 days of Grand List filing, file a **written grievance** with your town Listers. Orleans town deadlines vary:
- **Newport:** May 7 (2026)
- **Derby:** May 9 (2026)
- **Barton:** May 6 (2026)
- **Jay:** May 10 (2026)

**Your grievance must include:**
- Property identification (Grand List number, street address)
- Statement of alleged valuation or classification error
- Supporting evidence (comps, condition reports, income documentation)
- Requested assessed value

**We prepare and file your grievance.** You provide property details and supporting evidence.

### Step 3: Listers' Hearing (60–90 days after grievance)

Town Listers schedule a formal hearing. Your opportunity to present evidence; Listers must provide written findings.

### Step 4: Board of Civil Authority (BCA) Appeal (14 days after denial)

You have **14 days** to appeal to your town's **Board of Civil Authority** (select board).

**BCA standard:** You must prove by clear and convincing evidence that Listers' valuation is wrong.

### Step 5: Director of Property Valuation and Review (State Appeal)

If BCA denies your appeal, appeal to Vermont's **Director** (state level).

**State appeal standard:** De novo review; Director weighs evidence independently.

## Winning Grievance Evidence: What Persuades Listers & the BCA

### Comparable Sales Analysis (Most Powerful)

**Residential properties:** Find 3–5 recent arm's-length sales of similar properties in the same neighborhood, sold within 12 months. Adjust for square footage, age, condition, lot size, views, waterfront status, renovation recency.

**Orleans opportunity:** Lake Memphremagog waterfront properties are selling at 15–25% discounts from listed prices. Document listing-to-sale ratios; a property listed at $480K but sold at $395K directly contradicts a $445K assessment.

**Commercial properties:** Recent sales of similar hospitality, retail, or ski-adjacent properties are decisive. Limited comps data in Orleans makes any recent sale powerful evidence.

### Income and Expense Documentation

**For hospitality properties:**
- 2–3 years of actual tax returns (Schedule C, Form 1065)
- Detailed P&L statements (gross revenue, operating expenses, NOI)
- Bank statements documenting actual revenue
- Payroll records showing actual labor costs
- Utility bills showing seasonal variation

**For seasonal Jay Peak/waterfront hospitality:**
- Actual booking data showing occupancy rates (critical if <65%)
- Documented revenue by month (prove January ≠ July)
- Deferred maintenance lists and contractor estimates
- Management company agreements showing actual fees
- Weather/snow data proving correlation between ski conditions and occupancy

**For lake-based businesses:**
- Actual operating revenues showing seasonal pattern
- Documentation of environmental restrictions (water quality, shoreline protection)
- Documentation of weather-related operational limitations

### Professional Appraisals

A certified MAI appraisal (cost: $1,500–$3,500) carries tremendous weight. Ensure the appraiser has experience with ski-town, waterfront, and seasonal property valuations in rural Vermont.

### Condition and Physical Deficiency Reports

Document deferred maintenance:
- Roof age and replacement cost
- HVAC, plumbing, electrical issues
- Foundation or structural damage
- Water damage, moisture intrusion, ice damage
- Contractor quotes for necessary repairs

Example: A $350K waterfront property with $60K roof + $45K foundation work + $25K water damage = $220K market value, not $350K.

### Classification and Education Tax Documentation

For seasonal properties:
- Proof of actual primary residence status (voter registration, driver's license, insurance)
- Proof that property qualifies for homestead education tax rate
- Documentation of Lister's error in nonhomestead classification
