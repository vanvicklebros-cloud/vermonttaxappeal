## Franklin County: Vermont's Dairy Hub and Canadian Border Economy

Franklin County—Vermont's northernmost county—stretches from St. Albans (population 3,200) through the heart of Vermont dairy country, extending to the Canadian border at Swanton, Georgia, and Highgate. The county's Grand List exceeds $2.8 billion, with median property tax bills around $3,400–$4,600 annually.

Franklin County's economy is heavily agricultural and geographically constrained by its Canadian border position, creating unique assessment challenges:

- **Dairy industry in structural decline.** Vermont dairy exports have fallen 40%+ since 2010. Yet property valuations reflect historical commodity prices and farm income levels that no longer exist.
- **St. Albans as regional commercial hub.** St. Albans is the county's economic center (and regional hub for northeastern Vermont), but commercial properties are assessed with assumptions of growth that isn't materializing.
- **Ben & Jerry's and Cabot Cooperative legacy.** The county's two most famous agricultural-industrial brands (Ben & Jerry's ice cream, Cabot Creamery cheese) drive some employment and food-processing facility demand, but this base is stable-to-declining, not growing.
- **Canadian border logistics (overstated).** Properties near Canadian border crossings (I-89 at Highgate, local crossing at Swanton, St. Armand border station) are valued on assumptions of cross-border logistics demand that has not materialized post-2008.
- **Residential farmland with declining farm income.** Thousands of acres of farmland owned by farmers whose milk checks are inadequate to service debt. Farmland values are decoupling from agricultural income, yet assessments haven't caught up.

## Residential Overtaxation in Franklin County

### St. Albans: Regional Hub Premium Compression

St. Albans is Franklin County's only true commercial and residential hub. Residential properties are assessed with a regional-hub premium. Yet this premium has compressed as growth has stalled.

A St. Albans residential property (3-bedroom colonial, 1,700 SF, assessed $265,000 in 2023) is overvalued by 8–12% in 2026 market. Current comps (Feb–Mar 2026) show $230–$245K range. Assessment at $265K is 8–15% high.

Regional hub appeal is real but limited. St. Albans is modest-sized; growth is stalled.

### Farming Communities: Commodity Price-Tied Decline

Rural farming communities (Swanton, Georgia, Highgate, Sheldon, Franklin) have residential properties tied to farm income. As dairy economics have collapsed, residential property values in these communities have softened.

A farmhouse property in rural Franklin (3-bedroom colonial, 2,000 SF, on 50 acres, assessed $320,000) should be assessed $260–$290K. The farmland is not generating income; the property is residential-only. Listers apply agricultural land value to non-farmed parcels, inflating value by 10–15%.

### Border Towns: Cross-Border Commuter Limited Appeal

Swanton and other border towns (within 10 miles of Canadian border) were valued on cross-border commuter and logistics worker appeal. This premium doesn't exist materially.

A Swanton residential property (3-bedroom cape, 1,500 SF, assessed $210,000) should be assessed $185–$200K. Border proximity is neutral-to-negative (perceived risk, actual border restrictions). Assessment at $210K is 5–12% high.

## Commercial Property Overtaxation in Franklin County

### Dairy Facilities and Agricultural Processing: Income Collapse

Dairy farming's structural economics have collapsed. Milk prices have fallen from $20/cwt (2008) to $15/cwt (current), while feed and operational costs have remained sticky. This means farm NOI has collapsed, and dairy-related facilities should be valued far lower than historical cost basis.

**Example: Dairy farm with milking parlor and bulk tank infrastructure, assessed $580,000**

Lister's assumption (based on historical farm income):
- Farm milk production income: $125K/year
- Crop sales: $30K
- Government programs: $8K
- Total: $163K gross
- Operating expenses: $95K
- NOI: $68K
- Cap rate: 5% (agricultural, presumed stable)
- Assessed value: $1.36M

Current reality (2026):
- Farm milk production income (lower volume, lower prices): $85K
- Crop sales: $22K
- Government programs: $6K
- Total: $113K gross
- Operating expenses: $92K (feed and labor sticky)
- NOI: $21K
- Cap rate: 8% (agricultural, volatile, distressed)
- True value: $262,500
- Overvaluation: $1.097M (81%)

Properties near Canadian border crossings (I-89 at Highgate, local roads at Swanton) were valued on cross-border logistics and trade expectations post-NAFTA. This demand hasn't materialized as strongly as anticipated.

## The Vermont Grievance Process for Franklin County

### Grand List (April 1) → Lister Grievance (May 1)

Vermont law requires the Grand List to be filed by April 1. You have 30 days to file a written grievance.

**File with:**
- St. Albans: City Assessor, (802) 524-2510
- Swanton: Town Listers, (802) 868-7816
- Highgate: Town Listers, (802) 868-3591
- Sheldon: Town Listers, (802) 933-2349
- Georgia: Town Listers, (802) 524-9414
- Other towns: Contact town clerk

**Deadline:** May 1 (approximately)

**What to include:**
- Property address and ID number
- Assessed value
- Requested value with justification
- Supporting evidence (farm data, income analysis, comps, market data)

### Lister Hearing (June)

Listers schedule hearings by mid-June. For dairy/agricultural, bring Schedule F, milk production records, commodity price data. For commercial, bring income analysis and rent comparables.

### BCA Appeal (14 days after lister decision)

If unsatisfied, appeal to Board of Civil Authority within 14 days. BCA conducts site inspection and hearing. Success rate: 5–12% reductions with solid evidence.

### State Appeal (30 days after BCA decision)

Appeal to Director of Property Valuation and Review if necessary. Success rate: 40–50% with strong evidence.

## Evidence That Wins Grievances in Franklin County

### Dairy and Agricultural Facilities: Commodity Price and Income Data

For dairy farms and agricultural facilities, gather:
- Schedule F (IRS farm tax returns) for past 3 years
- Actual milk production records and milk price paid (DFA milk checks, milk marketing cooperative statements)
- USDA monthly dairy price data (showing $20/cwt → $15/cwt decline)
- Feed costs (showing cost pressures haven't abated)
- Herd size and productivity trends (showing no growth)

If Lister valued based on $18/cwt milk prices and current prices are $15/cwt (–17%), plus feed costs are up 15%, the farm's NOI has collapsed 40%+. Assessed value must reflect current economics.

**Example: Franklin County dairy farm, 150 head, assessed $680K**

Your actual 2024–2025 income:
- Milk revenue (150 head × 21,000 lbs/year × $15/cwt): $47,250
- Crops/forage sales: $8,200
- USDA support/insurance: $4,100
- Total: $59,550 gross
- Operating expenses: $48,000
- NOI: $11,550
- Cap rate: 8% (reflecting volatility)
- True value: $144,375

Lister assessed on cost approach (replacement cost of improvements + land value) at $680K. True income-based value is $144K. That's an 79% overvaluation.

### St. Albans Commercial: Rent Comparables and Occupancy Data

For St. Albans commercial properties, gather:
- Recent lease agreements (actual rents, not asking rates)
- Occupancy data (vacancies downtown, actual foot traffic)
- Comparable downtown retail rents from similar-sized VT cities (Montpelier, Barre, Rutland) showing lower rates
- St. Albans downtown economic trend data

St. Albans is not a booming retail destination. Rents should reflect a modest, stable market, not growth assumptions.

### Border Logistics: Market Reality Data

For border-adjacent properties, document:
- Actual lease rates for similar warehouse/logistics space
- Occupancy rates (not speculative assumptions)
- Cross-border trade data (showing modest growth post-2008, not boom)
- Comparable warehouse/logistics properties in less border-strategic locations showing lower values

Border proximity premium should not exceed 5–10% in actual market. Speculative "strategic border location" uplift is not justified.
