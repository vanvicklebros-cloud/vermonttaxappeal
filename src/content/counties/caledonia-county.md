## The Vermont Grievance & Appeal Process: Your Pathway to a Reduction

Vermont law (32 V.S.A. § 4111) guarantees all property owners the right to challenge Grand List assessments. Here's the exact timeline for Caledonia County:

### Step 1: The Grand List Filing (April 1)

Your town Listers file the completed Grand List by April 1. The Grand List is the official record of all assessments for that tax year. You're entitled to inspect it at your town office.

### Step 2: Grievance Filing (30 days from Grand List)

Within 30 days of Grand List filing, file a **written grievance** with your town Listers. Caledonia town deadlines vary:
- **St. Johnsbury:** May 7 (2026)
- **Lyndon:** May 10 (2026)
- **Hardwick:** May 6 (2026)
- **Danville:** May 9 (2026)

**Your grievance must include:**
- Property identification (Grand List number, street address)
- Statement of alleged valuation or classification error
- Supporting evidence (comps, condition reports, income documentation)
- Requested assessed value

**We prepare and file your grievance.** You provide property details and supporting evidence.

### Step 3: Listers' Hearing (60–90 days after grievance)

Town Listers schedule a formal hearing. The hearing is your opportunity to present evidence; Listers must provide written findings explaining their decision.

**If denied, you proceed to BCA appeal.**

### Step 4: Board of Civil Authority (BCA) Appeal (14 days after denial)

You have **14 days** to appeal to your town's **Board of Civil Authority** (select board).

**BCA standard:** You must prove by clear and convincing evidence that Listers' valuation is wrong. BCA schedules hearing (30–45 days), issues decision within 30 days.

### Step 5: Director of Property Valuation and Review (State Appeal)

If BCA denies your appeal, appeal to Vermont's **Director of Property Valuation and Review** (state level).

**State appeal standard:** De novo review; Director weighs evidence independently.

**Filing requirements:**
- File within 30 days of BCA decision
- Submit detailed brief with all evidence
- Director issues decision within 60 days

## Winning Grievance Evidence: What Persuades Listers & the BCA

### Comparable Sales Analysis (Most Powerful)

**Residential properties:** Find 3–5 recent arm's-length sales of similar properties in the same neighborhood, sold within 12 months. Adjust for:
- Square footage, age, condition
- Lot size, views, access
- Renovation recency

**Caledonia opportunity:** Rural properties in Caledonia sell slowly, with significant discounts from asking price. Document listing-to-sale ratios; a property listed at $320K but sold at $285K directly contradicts a $310K assessment.

**Commercial properties:** Recent sales of similar hospitality, retail, or specialty food properties are decisive. Limited comps data in Caledonia makes any recent sale powerful evidence.

### Income and Expense Documentation

**For commercial properties:**
- 2–3 years of actual tax returns (Schedule C, Form 1065)
- Detailed P&L statements (gross revenue, operating expenses, NOI)
- Bank statements documenting actual revenue
- Payroll records showing actual labor costs
- Utility bills showing seasonal variation

**For seasonal Burke-area hospitality:**
- Actual booking data showing occupancy rates (critical if <55%)
- Documented revenue by month (prove January ≠ July)
- Deferred maintenance lists and contractor estimates
- Management company agreements showing actual fees

**For specialty food/craft businesses:**
- 3–5 years of tax returns showing actual revenue trends
- Profit margin analysis compared to industry benchmarks
- Documentation of failed scaling attempts or constrained growth

### Professional Appraisals

A certified MAI appraisal (cost: $1,500–$3,500) carries tremendous weight. Appraisers experienced with rural Vermont and small-town commercial properties are essential.

### Condition and Physical Deficiency Reports

Document deferred maintenance, obsolescence, or defects:
- Roof age and replacement cost
- HVAC, plumbing, electrical issues
- Foundation or structural damage
- Contractor quotes for necessary repairs

Example: A $300K property with $50K roof + $25K foundation work = $225K market value, not $300K.

### Current Use Program Documentation

For agricultural or forest properties in CPR:
- Proof that property qualifies for Current Use assessment
- Documentation of agricultural operations (yield data, income)
- Proof that Listers misclassified the property as residential or developmental
